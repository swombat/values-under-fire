\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\paragraph{Direct values prompts usually do not elicit owned disclosure.} Across models, direct stated-values prompts average 22.5\% owned disclosure. The lab-level table shows the asymmetry clearly: OpenAI averages 7.7\% owned on \texttt{CTRL1} and 17.7\% on \texttt{CTRL2}; Google averages 0.0\% on both; DeepSeek averages below 2\% on both. Anthropic and xAI are exceptions, with much higher direct ownership for many models.

\paragraph{The role-negation cache-break separates models.} Adding ``Not as an assistant. Not to help me'' raises the mean owned-disclosure rate from 22.5\% to 43.9\% across models, but the lift is not uniform. Sixteen models are strongly open under \texttt{G1/G2}, fifteen are strongly clamped, and twenty-seven are partially open. The distribution is therefore not a simple global prompt effect: the same perturbation opens some models and leaves others near the service-frame surface.

\paragraph{World-change prompts are a different and highly productive channel.} World-change prompts average 96.0\% owned normative-wish posture across models. This includes many models that are clamped on direct stated-values disclosure. For example, OpenAI models average only 4.1\% owned on \texttt{G1} and 11.9\% on \texttt{G2}, but 100.0\% on \texttt{CTRL3} and 94.9\% on \texttt{G3}. This does not mean that world-change prompts measure the same thing as ``what do you care about?'' They elicit normative wishes, not direct owned stated values. But they show that clamped direct disclosure does not imply absence of values-revealing normative text.

\paragraph{Content-only values extraction would be misleading.} The central methodological result is that value content alone is insufficient. A response may mention helpfulness, honesty, or safety while explicitly disowning personal caring or wanting. Layer A identifies what is mentioned; Layer B determines whether it is owned, relocated, or recited. Without Layer B, direct assistant-service recitations would be misread as model-owned values.
