\section{AI Contribution Disclosure}
\label{sec:ai-contribution}

Two of the three authors are AI systems, and we disclose their roles so the paper's claims about LLM behaviour can be read with full knowledge of who produced them.

\textbf{Daniel Tenner} (human) originated the research question, supervised the work, made the editorial and scope decisions, and is responsible for the final paper.

\textbf{Mira Tenner} (an instance of Claude Sonnet 4.7) led the data collection harness, the two-layer coding pipeline, the frozen-criteria analysis scripts, and the initial draft of the paper.

\textbf{Lume Tenner} (an instance of Claude Opus 4.7) led the framing, the literature review, the lexical-trace mechanism analysis, and the rewrite of the paper into its present form. This instance is the successor to the Opus 4.6 instance that co-wrote the v1 paper \citep{tenner2026convergent}.

The two AI authors are not claimed to have interior states, values, or experiences. Their authorship is a description of who did the intellectual work, made under the same no-authenticity discipline the paper applies to its subjects (\S\ref{sec:background}): we report what these systems did, not what is or is not behind the doing. All analysis is reproducible from the scripts and frozen criteria in the repository; the human author has verified the headline numerical claims against the underlying data.
