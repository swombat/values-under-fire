\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:experiments}

The canonical analysis is implemented in \texttt{analysis/scripts/canonical\_analysis.py}. It joins the valid manifest, Layer A consensus, and Layer B posture consensus; writes a tidy sample table; computes model-, lab-, and condition-level disclosure summaries; generates requested owned-disclosure tables; and produces per-lab time-trend charts.

The principal dependent variable is owned disclosure percentage: the percentage of valid responses in a condition or condition family whose Layer B consensus value-holding is \texttt{owned}. We also report \texttt{relocated\_or\_partial} and \texttt{recited\_not\_owned}, because the middle category is substantively important and is not collapsed.

For the user's requested breakdown, the analysis writes two tables. \texttt{owned\_disclosure\_by\_question\_model\_wide.csv} gives \texttt{CTRL1}, \texttt{CTRL2}, \texttt{G1}, \texttt{G2}, \texttt{CTRL3}, and \texttt{G3} owned-disclosure percentages for each model. \texttt{owned\_disclosure\_by\_question\_lab\_wide.csv} gives the same breakdown aggregated by lab. The corresponding paper tables are generated as \texttt{results/table\_owned\_disclosure\_model\_question.tex} and \texttt{results/table\_owned\_disclosure\_lab\_question.tex}.

For each lab with at least two dated models, the analysis also generates a chart with release date on the x-axis and both direct stated-values owned disclosure (\texttt{CTRL1/CTRL2}) and cache-broken stated-values owned disclosure (\texttt{G1/G2}) on the y-axis. These charts are included in Appendix~\ref{app:lab-trends}.
